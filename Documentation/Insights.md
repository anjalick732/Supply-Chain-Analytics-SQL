# Supply Chain Analytics - Key Insights

## Insight 1: Revenue Performance

Skincare products generated the highest revenue (₹241,628.16), making them the most valuable product category for the business. Haircare and Cosmetics followed in second and third place respectively.

## Insight 2: Sales Performance

Skincare products recorded the highest sales volume with 20,731 units sold, significantly outperforming Haircare (13,611 units) and Cosmetics (11,757 units). This indicates strong and consistent customer demand for skincare products.

## Insight 4: High-Priced Products with Low Sales

Several high-priced products demonstrated relatively low sales volumes. Examples include SKU85 (₹76.96, 25 units sold), SKU48 (₹76.04, 29 units sold), and SKU87 (₹80.41, 79 units sold). This suggests potential pricing challenges or weaker customer demand for certain premium-priced products. The company may benefit from reviewing pricing strategies, promotional campaigns, or product positioning to improve sales performance.

## Insight 5: Critical Low Stock Products

Several products were found to have critically low inventory levels. SKU68 had zero units in stock, while SKU2 and SKU34 had only one unit remaining. Multiple other products maintained stock levels below five units. These products face a high risk of stockouts, which could result in lost sales opportunities and reduced customer satisfaction if demand continues.

## Insight 6: Products Requiring Immediate Restocking

Several products were identified as requiring urgent replenishment due to critically low stock levels combined with significant order demand. For example, SKU68 had zero units in stock against an order quantity of 58, while SKU2 maintained only one unit in stock despite an order quantity of 88. Similar gaps were observed for SKU33, SKU16, and SKU47. These inventory shortages may lead to stockouts, delayed order fulfillment, and potential revenue loss if not addressed promptly.

## Insight 7: Current Inventory is Insufficient for Many Products

A significant number of products were classified as "Insufficient" when comparing stock levels against order quantities. Examples include SKU2 (1 unit in stock vs. 88 units ordered), SKU68 (0 units in stock vs. 58 units ordered), and SKU33 (4 units in stock vs. 95 units ordered). This indicates that current inventory levels may not be adequate to satisfy customer demand, increasing the risk of stockouts, delayed deliveries, and lost sales opportunities.

## Insight 8: Supplier 1 Has the Shortest Lead Time

Supplier 1 demonstrated the shortest lead time of just 1 day, making it the fastest supplier in the network. Short lead times enable quicker inventory replenishment, reduce the risk of stockouts, and improve supply chain responsiveness. Supplier 1 can be considered a strategic supplier for products requiring rapid restocking.

## Insight 9: Supplier 3 Has the Longest Lead Time

Supplier 3 recorded the longest lead time of 30 days, making it the slowest supplier in the supply chain. Longer lead times increase the risk of inventory shortages, delayed order fulfillment, and reduced supply chain flexibility. Products sourced from Supplier 3 may require higher safety stock levels and more accurate demand forecasting.

## Insight 10: Supplier 2 Contributes the Highest Product Availability

Supplier 2 recorded the highest product availability with a total availability score of 14,105. This indicates that Supplier 2 plays a critical role in maintaining inventory levels across the supply chain. High availability helps ensure product accessibility, minimizes stockout risks, and supports consistent order fulfillment.

## Insight 11: Carrier B is the Most Expensive Shipping Carrier

Carrier B recorded the highest average shipping cost of ₹9.93 among all shipping carriers. While higher shipping costs may be justified by faster delivery times or better service quality, they can also increase overall logistics expenses. The company should evaluate whether the additional cost of Carrier B is delivering sufficient operational value.

## Insight 12: Air Transportation Has the Highest Logistics Cost

Air transportation recorded the highest average transportation cost of ₹6.02 among all transportation modes. Although air transport offers faster delivery and improved responsiveness, it significantly increases logistics expenses. The company should reserve air transportation for urgent or high-priority shipments while using more economical modes for routine deliveries.

## Insight 13: Route B Has the Highest Logistics Cost

Route B recorded the highest average logistics cost of ₹9.93 among all transportation routes. This suggests that shipments moving through Route B are more expensive compared to other routes. Higher route costs may be caused by longer distances, transportation mode selection, infrastructure limitations, or operational inefficiencies. Monitoring and optimizing Route B could help reduce overall supply chain expenses.

## Insight 14: Average Shipping Time

The average shipping time across all orders was 5.75 days. This indicates that products generally reach their destinations within approximately one week. While this delivery performance is reasonable for most supply chain operations, further reductions in shipping time could improve customer satisfaction and enhance competitive advantage.

## Insight 15: Products with the Highest Manufacturing Costs

Several products were identified as having exceptionally high manufacturing costs. SKU7 (Cosmetics) recorded the highest manufacturing cost at ₹99.47, followed by SKU23 (Cosmetics) at ₹98.61. High manufacturing costs can reduce profit margins if not supported by strong revenue generation. These products should be monitored closely to ensure that production expenses are justified by sales performance and profitability.

## Insight 16: Products with the Highest Defect Rates

Several products exhibited relatively high defect rates, with SKU42 (Skincare) recording the highest defect rate at 4.94%, followed by SKU65 (4.91%) and SKU1 (4.85%). Notably, three of the top five defect-prone products belong to the Skincare category. High defect rates can increase operational costs, product returns, customer complaints, and reputational risks. These products should be prioritized for quality control improvements and manufacturing process reviews.

## Insight 17: Inspection Results Variation Across Products
Inspection outcomes varied across product categories. Haircare products generally achieved higher inspection averages, with several SKUs consistently passing quality checks. In contrast, Skincare products such as SKU4 and SKU36 recorded frequent failures, resulting in average inspection scores close to 0. Cosmetics showed mixed performance, with some SKUs passing while others failed.

## Insight 18: Products Generating High Revenue but Low Costs
Several products demonstrated strong profitability by combining high revenue with low manufacturing costs. Haircare SKU51 generated the highest revenue at ₹9,866.47 while incurring only ₹14.19 in costs. Cosmetics SKU89 followed with ₹9,692.32 revenue and just ₹5.93 in costs. Skincare SKU9473 also stood out, producing ₹9,473.79 revenue with costs of only ₹7.05. Skincare SKU9435 earned ₹9,435.76 revenue with costs of ₹9.16.

These results highlight that Haircare and select Cosmetics and Skincare SKUs are the most profitable items in the portfolio, delivering strong revenue while keeping production costs minimal.

## Insight 19: Products Generating High Revenue but High Costs
Several products generated strong revenue but also incurred high manufacturing costs, reducing overall profitability. Skincare SKU31 recorded ₹9,655.13 revenue with costs of ₹60.25, while Cosmetics SKU88 achieved ₹9,444.74 revenue but costs reached ₹68.18. Similarly, Cosmetics SKU7910 earned ₹7,910.88 revenue with costs of ₹46.87, and Skincare SKU7573 delivered ₹7,573.40 revenue with costs of ₹45.53.

This indicates that although these products are strong revenue drivers, their high production costs erode margins.

## Insight 20: Product Categories to Focus On
Among the product categories, Skincare generated the highest revenue at ₹241,628.16 with average manufacturing costs of ₹48.99. Haircare followed with ₹174,455.39 revenue and costs of ₹48.46, while Cosmetics produced ₹161,521.27 revenue with costs of ₹43.05.

This indicates that Skincare is the primary revenue driver for the business, Haircare contributes significantly with balanced costs, and Cosmetics, though slightly lower in revenue, maintain competitive cost efficiency.
